Mercenary deterrence: TVL to add before the flip

A farmer enters when their pro-rata emissions beat the market hurdle plus their round-trip price impact: entering means buying the kVCM leg through this thin pool and exiting means selling it back, a round trip costing ≈ F² ÷ depth. Amortized over their patience that friction covers a ≈ $65,179/yr slice of emissions with almost no TVL; every emission dollar beyond it needs 1 ÷ hurdle dollars of staked TVL. This stays consistent as TVL grows (unlike a fixed vAPR ceiling, since the friction premium shrinks with depth). Historically that friction, more than the displayed vAPR, is what kept capital out. Farmers below the threshold are not worth deterring — their supply-bounded background is priced as the leak row in the income table instead.

Assumptions
  • Emissions to a gauge = total gauge emissions × pool votes / total votes. Only 45% of Minter weekly (8,969,150 AERO) reaches gauges — the veAERO rebase and team allocation are carved out — so the total is measured by summing every live gauge's streaming rate (4,031,192 AERO/wk).
  • One-epoch lag: votes cast now direct emissions that start streaming at the next flip. The gauge currently streams 2,192 AERO/wk (set by last epoch's votes); current votes imply 2,173 AERO/wk at the next flip.
  • Total votes are held constant: reallocating our votes moves them between pools without changing the total.
  • Own-pool fees+bribes use the latest epoch's $10.50 (trailing 4-epoch average $7.28); voting more for our own pool doesn't create new fees, it only changes our share of them.
  • The comparison baseline is the proportional strategy (power split across the top-5 bluechip/stable pools by fees+bribes), not the votes as currently cast; other-pool voting income in the scenario spreads the non-allocated votes across those same baseline pools in the same proportions.
  • The farmer-equilibrium column is the worst case: farmers of the reference size/patience enter until the marginal one only nets the market hurdle, so staked TVL rises to the deterrence level and our LP emissions capture is diluted to roughly hurdle × our stake; voting income is unaffected. The deterred column is the best case (no entry); reality depends on how much deterrence TVL is posted before the flip.
  • Historical inflows that were never substantially withdrawn and have been staked at least 8 weeks are classified as sticky (passive capital parking, e.g. the small epoch-141 stake) and excluded from the mercenary evidence — they respond to something other than vAPR. The gauge's first epoch is likewise excluded: its flows were part of the token launch.
  • Small-farmer background: farmers below the deterrence threshold are priced, not deterred — their aggregate stake is bounded by the supply of small capital hunting this gauge, not by yield indifference. The background is a historical measurement, not a model: from the full per-address LP-transfer history (195 external stake episodes; entries during the launch week excluded as launch behavior, not yield-chasing), the most capital farmers below each size ever had staked at once was <$2k → $13k · <$5k → $33k · <$10k → $48k · <$15k → $84k · <$20k → $102k · <$25k → $120k · <$50k → $148k · <$100k → $323k (unbounded: $886k), with the peaks in the high-emission era of January 2026. Changing the threshold auto-fills the background by interpolating this curve; a future kVCM narrative could exceed it, so it stays editable. The leak row reduces our capture to our stake ÷ (our stake + background); the background is floored at the external stake currently present, so setting it to zero recovers the old static model. The threshold hint also shows the scenario's optimum — the smallest size the configured staked TVL already deters, from inverting the deterrence condition at S = staked after deposit: raising the threshold past it only adds leak, lowering it demands TVL the scenario hasn't posted.
  • Incumbent staked TVL is assumed static; in practice mercenary TVL chases displayed vAPR — that response is exactly what the deterrence calculator is for.
  • Break-even holding assumes the farmer buys the kVCM leg through this pool and sells it back on exit at similar depth (constant-product impact ≈ F²/depth for the round trip), earns their pro-rata emissions share, and pays no swap fees (ignoring them is conservative — fees would lengthen the break-even, and ≈99% of them flow back to us as the pool's dominant voter). kVCM sourced elsewhere only moves the price impact to that venue.
  • Prices are point-in-time (AERO $0.5564, kVCM $0.0304); no price-impact modeling for acquiring kVCM or for AERO sell pressure from farming.
  • LP deposits into a vAMM pool must be balanced 50/50 by value at deposit time, so the kVCM/USDC split cannot be chosen: half the deposit's value must be provided as each token (the scenario shows the implied amounts at current prices).
  • Cross-sectional hurdle: emissions-vAPR = gauge streaming rate × 52.14 weeks × AERO price ÷ staked TVL, over live gauges streaming emissions. Tokens (and AERO) are valued at the latest epoch flip from the committed prices.csv — deterministic between runs, refreshed when epochs close — with live Alchemy spot only for tokens outside its tracked-pool universe; gauges with unpriceable tokens are skipped. Pool "type" from LpSugar (≤ 0 = basic AMM, > 0 = CL).
  • Historical ceiling: pool-history.csv snapshots gauge state 1h after each epoch flip via archive calls; the AERO rate per epoch comes from the gauge's own rewardRateByEpoch record. Our TVL is the combined gauge balance of our addresses (the veAERO voter and the treasury LP address 0xf63a…) — LP staked by anyone else counts as external.
  • Deterrence TVL S solves annualized emissions = hurdle × S + allowance × S ÷ (S + U) exactly, where allowance = 52.1 × F ÷ H is the reference farmer's amortized round trip and U = $11,329 is the unstaked reserve depth (swap depth = S + U). The friction term is nearly depth-independent because the premium such a farmer demands (52.1·F/(depth·H)) falls as fast as TVL grows. Ignoring the farmer's own dilution errs on the safe side. Farmers smaller than F face little friction but take no meaningful emissions share; the sized-inflow record (external capital of consequence only ever arrived above 133% displayed vAPR) is the empirical sanity check. A starting point, not a hard rule.
